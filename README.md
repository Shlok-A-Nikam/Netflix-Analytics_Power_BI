## 1. Overview
The Netflix Analytics Dashboard provides a professional summary of the content library available on the platform as of 2021. It supports data-driven decisions by revealing insights into content trends, geographic distribution, and overall library composition (Movies vs. TV Shows).

## 2. Key Metrics
The dashboard includes key metrics that capture the composition of the Netflix library:
•	Total Movies: 6131
•	Total TV Shows: 2676
•	Total Countries Represented: 747
•	Oldest Release Year in Data: 1925
•	Latest Release Year in Data: 2021
•	Movies Percentage: 69.62%
•	TV Shows Percentage: 30.38%

## 3. Key Insights
•	Content Dominance: Movies significantly dominate the library, accounting for over two-thirds of the total content (69.62%).
•	Geographic Focus: The United States has the highest volume of content, followed by India.
•	Growth Trend: Content release trends show a massive surge in new titles added around the year 2017.
•	Rating Distribution: The TV-MA (Mature Audiences) rating is the most prevalent rating across the entire library.
•	Country-Specific Content: While the US leads overall, the mix of movies vs. TV shows varies by country; for example, India has a high proportion of movies.

## 4. Visuals and Analysis
The dashboard incorporates various visuals for detailed analysis:
•	KPIs (Univariate Analysis): Cards display the raw counts for movies, TV shows, countries, start year, and end year.
•	Donut Chart (Bivariate Analysis): Compares the count of Movie vs. TV Show types.
•	Line Chart (Bivariate Analysis): Tracks the Total Movies & TV-Shows by release year.
•	Tree Map (Bivariate Analysis): Visualizes the Top 10 countries by Movies & tv-shows size using rectangular areas proportional to the count.
•	Stacked Column Chart (Multivariate Analysis): Compares Country vs Type, showing the breakdown of movies and TV shows for specific countries.

## 5. Slicer Interaction
Slicers are present for filtering data by Release Year (using start and end year inputs). Users can dynamically filter all visuals to focus on specific segments of the content library.

## 6. Conclusion
The Netflix Analytics Dashboard consolidates key metrics and visual insights into one unified report. By integrating dynamic filters and clear visuals, it empowers users to efficiently analyze content performance and distribution across the platform.
